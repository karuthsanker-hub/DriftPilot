## What happened

Day 2 was the **bug-discovery day**. Three live bugs surfaced under load that the unit tests hadn't caught:

### Bug 1: `unrealized_pct=0` in signal scope (load-bearing)

**Effect:** signal's `evaluate_exit` always saw 0% gain/loss because `getattr(position, "current_price", entry_price)` returned `entry_price` (no `current_price` field on `PositionRecord`).

**Consequence A (early in day):** profit_take and stop_loss never fired. With trailing stop enabled, `trailing_stop` fired whenever `peak вүҘ activation + distance` вҖ” regardless of actual price. **The broker happened to fill at the live mid (which was at peak)**, so we accidentally exited near peak. This made LTH (+$291), ALKS (+$92), FDP (+$58) look like trailing-stop wins. **They were lucky bug outcomes.**

**Consequence B (after fix):** with the fix, signal correctly saw the real unrealized %. Stop_loss fired immediately on positions that had drifted past вҲ’1.5%. **A cluster of 4 stop_losses fired in the next 15 seconds** (AGCO вҲ’$270, CMI вҲ’$56, SPHR вҲ’$751, ROK вҲ’$122) вҖ” these were positions the buggy logic had been holding because `unrealized=0` masked their drawdowns.

### Bug 2: Per-symbol cap was per-active-position, not per-day

**Effect:** `MAX_TRADES_PER_SYMBOL_PER_DAY=1` was supposed to prevent re-buying the same symbol after exit. But the slot allocator only checked open positions, not closed-today positions. Once a position closed and freed the slot, the same catalyst event re-bought.

**Consequence:** SPHR was bought **4 times today** on the same news headline. ROK 2x, ALKS 2x. Each re-buy entered a deeper drawdown:
- SPHR-1: +$588 (peak-grab via Bug 1)
- SPHR-2: вҲ’$751
- SPHR-3: вҲ’$776
- SPHR-4: вҲ’$658

Net SPHR damage: **вҲ’$1,597** on a single news event.

### Bug 3: Sequential exit processing (caught yesterday but worth repeating)

**Effect:** monitor processed positions one-by-one, ~30s Г— N. With 9 positions, total cycle time ~4.5 min. By the time monitor reached position #5, the others had aged past `max_hold_minutes=60`, so time_stop fired instead of profit_take/stop_loss.

**Fix shipped today:** `asyncio.gather` over per-position tasks. Total cycle time now ~5s regardless of count.
